JEJAKA BLUE & GADIS HAZEL

ARIASEVA

‘ TOUCH HER AND I'LL KILL YOU’

Harta, kuasa, darjat… semua di hujung jarinya. Nampak gah pada zahir, namun batinnya memendam rasa yang kelam. Ada bahagia, tetapi tiada ketenangan di jiwa, sehinggalah seseorang mengusik pandangan mata birunya…
"Awak jangan curiga pasal saya. Saya bukan gengster. Saya manusia biasa juga"- ARYAN FROY LOUISE
Dia hanya seorang gadis polos yang cantik dan masih mencari hala tuju hidup. Tetapi, dia terlalu jual mahal sehingga tidak pernah tertarik dengan mana-mana lelaki, kecuali dia...
"Please... Awak jangan fikir awak handsome, awak boleh dapat apa yang awak nak. Dengan perempuan lain maybe ya, tapi tidak dengan saya. Saya warning awak ya, jangan pernah cuba untuk cuba. Did you get it?" - QUIN AVRIN
Perkenalan ringkas, namun terbias. Niat untuk berkawan akhirnya menjadi kenyataan.
"I don't kill people" - ARYAN FROY LOUISE
"But I do" - ARIQ FRANDY LOUISE
Bermandikan darah umpama perkara biasa. Bermain senjata api memang hobinya. Kematian dan semua itu dianggap percuma selagi namanya manusia. Tetapi, manusia sepertinya jua perlukan cinta. Layakkah seorang lelaki sepertinya memberi bunga indah kepada si mata hazel? Atau si mata hazel hanya mampu merasa detak tanpa tahu siapa sebenarnya jejaka blue itu? Antara suka dan duka, yang mana melingkari hidup mereka? Belajar daripada kehilangan, membawa penyesalan. Belajar daripada kehidupan, membawa kebenaran.


Genre: Romance
Pages: 550
Published: Jun 1, 2022
Publisher: Fajar Pakeer
Language: Malay

I honestly didn’t expect to get so attached to this book, but Jejaka Blue & Gadis Hazel really pulled me in. From the moment Aryan appeared with his mysterious blue eyes and that quiet, protective vibe, I was hooked. There’s something so intense yet soft about how he loves. It’s not loud, but it’s deep. It’s not just about possession but it shows how far he’d go to protect her, even if he never says it out loud. Quin, on the other hand, felt so relatable. She’s strong, a bit stubborn, but deep down, she’s just someone trying to find her place in the world. I could see parts of myself in her. I love how their story isn't rushed, it builds slowly, and I can feel the tension, the trust, the hurt, and the healing between them. What made me love this book even more is how real the emotions are. It talks about grief, trauma, and healing in a way that doesn’t feel forced. It’s not your typical sweet love story but it’s raw, messy, but beautiful. I cried a little, smiled a lot, and finished the last page feeling like I just lived their journey with them.
